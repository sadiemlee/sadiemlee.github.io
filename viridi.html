The Viridi Stella: 
Sustainable Luxury & Wellness Hotel Concept

experience design · Interior design
Attending the Ecole Hôtelière de Lausanne Digital Academy in 2021, we were tasked with developing a hotel concept of any kind. This involved determining the driving vision and purpose of the hotel, identifying aspirational clientele, researching current trends in the industry, and creating a mock-up of our hotel's design.

Some of our initial ideas & inspiration
Since the digital academy was hosted by EHL, a Swiss hospitality school, we wanted to pay homage to Swiss culture. With research, we found that many hotels in Switzerland emphasize more traditional aspects.
moodboard & brainstorming
This guided our decision to focus on Swiss values such as peacefulness, appreciation of nature, pleasure, and autonomy (swisscareersurvey.ch)
To add value to guests which would then add value to the business, we looked at different trends such as personalization, wellness, and sustainability, threading them together.
solution
a luxury eco-hotel in the Swiss Alps
a meditative space for holistic wellness
innovation in tech & unique experiences
a deep commitment to sustainability
Although our concept plays on some of the trends identified in research, it is grounded in its geographical location and the values of that location. The concept's name is derived from Latin, the origins of Italian, which is one of Switzerland's official languages. Spoken in the Southern region of the Alps and where we chose to place our hotel, "viridi" means "blooming" and "stella" means "point of light in a jewel," as we hoped to reflect the essence of the hotel concept we created.
guest journey (2023 revisit)
Features of Viridi Stella such as 3D printed gastronomy, circular sustainability (converting food waste to biofuel), and customizable holistic wellness packages combine together to create a unique, highly personalized experience. Such an experience creates a high level of interaction, building strong guest relationships which ultimately translates into loyalty and a stable customer network.

Our team had two days to ideate and flesh out this concept, so channels for future growth include developing a more solidified business plan and branding strategy, as well as identifying risks and barriers to entry, costs and funding, and guest personas.
implications & future growth
Reflections on the project and process
For this project, I learned a bit of interior design and how to make renderings (with a home design tool—Homestyler 3D!). I also learned experience and service design through workshops that were provided through the program as well as on my own online, and how these can be drivers of business.

I also had the great opportunity to work with an international team—hailing from Argentina, Mexico, and across the United States, plus mentors from Switzerland, Hong Kong, and the UAE. Collaborating across language (and oftentimes slang) barriers had a learning curve, but proved to be a incredibly valuable and enjoyable experience.
