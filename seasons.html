the challenge
Working in teams, we were given the following case problem: 
"How can we use technology to personalize fashion-retail while enhancing the e-commerce experience?"
1. Looking for more niche forms of personalization for differentiation
2. Seeing color seasons as a major trend on TikTok
3. Having difficulty figuring out mode of delivery
A common form of personalization, particularly in retail, is recommendation systems based on similar products. To provide Aritzia with a competitive advantage, however, we looked to see how we could augment this industry standard already in place.
We then wanted to incorporate color seasons into a recommendation system and were running through ideas like a smart mirror, video capture, and image capture but came across barriers of use such as accessibility and privacy.
RESEARCH & PROCESS
Originating from Korea, the idea of color seasons is to find the color(s) that best compliment your physical attributes such as skin tone and hair color. While typically a costly in-person service, TikTok created filters that allowed users to see the range of color seasons.
color seasons on tiktok
solution
Real-time web capture with skin tone detection analyzed through a classification model to match a seasonal palette and recommend Aritzia products
We felt that this solution would provide an interesting form of personalization, and would be the most feasible given the 2-day time constraint.
user flow
implications
In adding value to users with the novel experience, value is also added to Aritzia as the company by allowing for a competitive advantage.
Personalization also drives customer engagement, increasing sales, ultimately building loyal customer relationships which in turn drives retention rates.
INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPING
- Combat racial bias in the classification model
- Further personalize the color seasons matched to users
- Actually program and deploy the algorithm & classification model
Since humans are biased, our datasets also often reflect biases. By continuing to train the classification model with greater diversity in the training data, we can reduce racial bias in the overall experience.
Since my team was in the beginner stream of the hackathon, we had limited programming experience. Thus, we were only able to build mockups & prototypes, and frameworks for both the skin detection algorithm and classification model.
future growth · time & experience constraints
The frameworks we created were primitive in that they did only accounted for skin tone as a physical attribute; it did not consider other characteristics such as undertones and hair color.
Technically, I learned a lot from this project-learning HTML & CSS which was new to me, as well as making more complex prototypes with interaction in Figma. Perhaps more importantly, however, I also learned a tremendous amount about collaboration with a team whom you've never met before: learning to be open to new ideas and different perspectives, which ultimately gives us a greater opportunity to ideate freely and take aspects from each person's viewpoint to create a concept that reflects both our individuality and cohesion as a team.
