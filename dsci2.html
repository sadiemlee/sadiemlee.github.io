The retail industry has experienced a major shift towards e-commerce over the past several years, especially with the pandemic as people lost the ability to go to traditional brick-and-mortar stores. To support businesses looking to gain insight into the market, we can leverage the immense amounts of data, perform analysis, and turn findings into meaningful information.
Our project worked to answer the questions:
"Can we predict whether a consumer will purchase a product on an e-commerce site based on specific site factors?
‍
If so, what are the factors that allow us to create the most optimal predictive model?"

The best  predictors of revenue were found to be “PageValues” (number of webpages the user visited before making an ecommerce transaction), “Administrative_Duration” (total amount of time spent on webpages containing administrative data), “ProductRelated” (type of webpage), “ExitRates” (percentage that the wepage was the last one visited during a session) and “Informational” (type of webpage). The model had an 89.2% accuracy level, as shown in Table 1.6, which was the highest among other predictors tested.

By analyzing all of the numerical values in the dataset, we were able to understand the connections between the variables and how they interact to influence online consumer purchasing intentions. Through this analysis, we hope to help improve e-commerce by illustrating to brands the factors that should be focused on when designing an e-commerce site's user experience and interface. Furthermore, this analysis can help brands predict consumer purchasing intentions and thus level of revenue generated, ultimately growing the brand and increasing profits.
