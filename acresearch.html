COURSEWORK (COGNITIVE SYSTEMS 200) • MACHINE LEARNING & PSYCHOLOGY
Artificial intelligence has become increasingly prevalent in nearly all sectors, from digital products to physical products. One of the major areas where further study is needed is in affective computing, and more specifically, emotion recognition. ER has had difficulty in that it cannot be solely modeled off of human cognition: humans ourselves aren’t perfect at identifying and interpreting emotions. Similarly, modeling emotion recognition off of behavioral cues then leads to issues like masked or mixed emotions.

Currently in the exploration stage, our primary exploratory research question is "How can we improve emotion recognition so that it is not solely reliant on behavioral cues?" We are looking into different avenues, the foremost (as of present) being measuring physiological signals through wearable biosensors and then applying machine learning techniques to create correspondence between emotions and such signals to determine emotional states. This study has implications for systems where emotion recognition is beneficial, our focus being a healthcare companion for children in hospitals.
