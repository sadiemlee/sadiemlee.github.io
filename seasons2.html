Looking for more niche forms of personalization for differentiation
Working in teams, we were given the following case problem: “How can we use technology to personalize fashion-retail while enhancing the e-commerce experience?”
A common form of personalization, particularly in retail, is recommendation systems based on similar products. To provide Aritzia with a competitive advantage, we looked to see how we could augment this industry standard already in place.
03
Seeing color seasons as a major trend on TikTok
04
Having difficulty figuring out the ideal mode of delivery
Originating from Korea, the  idea of color seasons is to identify the color(s) that best compliment your physical attributes like skin tone & hair color.  While typically a costly in-person service, TikTok has created filters that enable users to view the range of color seasons.
We then wanted to incorporate color seasons into a product recommendation system and ran through ideas such as a smart mirror, video upload, and image upload, but came across barriers of acquisition and use such as cost, accessibility, and privacy.

– Combat racial bias in the classification model
Since people are biased, our datasets also often reflect biases. By continuing to train the classification model with greater diversity in the training data, we can reduce racial bias in the overall experience.
– Further personalize the color seasons for users
The frameworks we created were primitive in that they only accounted for skin tone as a physical attribute; given our time constraints we were unable to use other characteristics such as undertones and hair color in the model.
– Actually program & deploy the algorithm & model
Since our team was in the beginner stream, this meant that we had limited programming experience. Thus, we were only able to build mock-ups & prototypes, and frameworks for both the skin detection algorithm and neural network.
